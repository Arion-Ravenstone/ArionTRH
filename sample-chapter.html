A Glimpse into Arion's World

“ Excerpt from Chapter Six:”

The trees didn’t whisper. They hissed.

Twisted things, all charred bark and knotted roots, clawed at Arion’s ankles as he pushed deeper into the cursed grove. The silence wasn’t silence—it was held breath, stretched to a breaking point.

His sword pulsed in his hand like a second heartbeat, cold and steady. Not comfort. Just presence.

He didn't believe in comfort anymore.

The sky above was ash. The ground below, bones.

He stopped. A rustle behind him.

Then—nothing.

Then—her.

She stepped from between two dead oaks like she'd always belonged to the dark.

Lean. Wild-eyed. Gold irises ringed in blood.

Her blades gleamed like promises he didn’t ask for.

She didn’t speak. She watched.

Arion raised his weapon, voice like gravel and ghosts.

“Who the hell are you?”

Her smile didn’t reach her eyes.

“Someone who remembers what you were before the gods broke you.”

A pause.

Then—

“Let’s see what’s left.”
